How to Care for Moringa Plants

Materials Needed

  • Germinated Moringa seeds
  • Water
  • Planting pots or garden bed
  • Well-draining soil

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Plant the Seeds in Soil:

After germination, plant your Moringa seeds in well-drained soil in a sunny location.

2. Water Regularly:

Water the seedlings lightly, ensuring the soil is moist but not waterlogged.

3. Fertilization:

Add compost or organic fertilizer every couple of months to nourish the plant.

4. Pruning:

Once the tree is 4 feet tall, prune it regularly to promote lateral branching and encourage a bushy structure.

5. Sunlight:

Moringa trees thrive in full sunlight. Make sure they receive at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight per day.

6. Pest Control:

Check for pests, though Moringa is naturally resistant. Use organic pest control methods if necessary.

7. Harvesting:

Once the tree reaches 6-8 feet, you can start harvesting the leaves for food or health products.
